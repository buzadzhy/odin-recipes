Borshch

Description

Borshch is a traditional Ukrainian dish. It's a boiled meat based soup usually served with grey bread, garlic and Ukrainian salty beacon called 'salo'. While recipes may differ from region to region and even from home to home, classic borshch has 3 common ingredients which are cabbage, beetroot and tomato pasta.

Ingredients

Ingredients for 3.5 - 4 liters of water:

  • beef or pork - 600-700 g
  • beets - 500 g
  • potatoes - 500 g
  • carrots - 200 g
  • onion - 200 g
  • tomatoes - 300 g or 3 tbsp. tomato paste
  • cabbage - 300 g
  • garlic - 3-4 cloves of garlic
  • bay leaf - 3-4 pcs.
  • vinegar - 1-2 tsp.
  • salt, pepper

Steps

  1. Cut the meat and boil the meat for 1.5 hours.
  2. Chop the onion (and tomatoes if you have those), grate the carrots and beets.
  3. Fry the onions in sunflower oil for light-golden color.
  4. Add carrots to the onions, fry for a few minutes.
  5. Add tomatoes or pasta, vinegar and simmer for 5-7 minutes.
  6. Chop potatoes and add to the boiling broth.
  7. Add beets to the boiling broth.
  8. Shred the cabbage and add to the boiling broth, cook for 5 minutes.
  9. Add fried vegetables.
  10. Add bay leaf, salt and pepper.
  11. Boil until the cabbage gets soft.
  12. Squeeze the garlic through a garlic press, cover and remove from heat.
  13. Let it steep for 20-30 minutes, the borshch is ready.
  14. Serve the dish with grey bread, "salo", garlic and green onion.
